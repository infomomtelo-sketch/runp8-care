Title22 · Demo walkthrough · ~15 minutes

From binders to one screen: presenting Title22

A step-by-step script for walking anyone — a fellow administrator, a licensee, an investor — through the whole app. Each step tells you where to click, what to say, and why it matters.

Before you demo — 10 minutes of prep

  • Use a demo account with data already in it: 2–3 residents, 2 staff members with cert dates, a few medications, one incident, some checklist items done, a Daily Log entry or two, a couple of trainings. An empty app demos badly.
  • Set one staff certification to expire within 30 days — the warning it triggers is a demo moment.
  • Open title22.app in a clean browser tab, already signed in. Never make an audience watch you log in.
  • Charge the phone/laptop. Have cell data as backup if the venue Wi-Fi is bad.
01

Open with the problem, not the app

1 min
Say"How do you get ready for a DSS inspection today? For most facilities it's three-ring binders, sticky notes, and hoping nothing expired. Title22 puts your whole facility — staff, residents, medications, incidents, daily charting — on one screen, and it tells you what's out of compliance before the analyst finds it."

Why this order: administrators buy relief from inspection anxiety, not software. Name the fear first; the app is the answer.

02

Dashboard & the AI daily briefing

2 min
Sign in→Dashboard

Do: Let the AI briefing load on screen. Point at it. Then point at the stat cards.

Say"Every morning this briefing reads your actual facility data — overdue tasks, expiring certifications, recent incidents — and tells you what needs attention today. You didn't ask it anything. It's already working."

Why it lands: this is the "wow" in the first 60 seconds. AI that reads their data beats AI that chats.

03

Residents

1 min
Menu→🏠 Residents

Do: Open one resident. Show the profile fields.

Say"Every resident's record in one place — admission info, physician, emergency contacts. Everything else in the app connects back to a resident."
04

Staff & certifications

2 min
Menu→👥 Staff

Do: Open the staff member whose cert expires soon. Show the expiration warning.

Say"First aid, LiveScan, mandated reporter — with expiration dates. When something's 30 days out, the app flags it. This is the exact question an analyst asks: 'show me current certifications for everyone on shift.' Now the answer is one tap."
05

Training tracker

1 min
Menu→🎓 Training

Do: Show the annual-hours progress bars, then log a training live — it takes 15 seconds.

Say"Every direct-care staff member needs 20 hours of in-service training a year. These bars show exactly where each person stands — green means done, red means get them scheduled. And logging a training takes seconds."
06

Medications & the MAR

2 min
Menu→💊 Medicationsthen📋 MAR

Do: Show a resident's medication list, then mark one dose given on the MAR.

Say"Medication records are the single most scrutinized document in an inspection. The MAR here auto-fills from the medication list, so staff document each dose in two taps. And one deliberate design choice: the AI never suggests or comments on dosages — that stays between the physician and the pharmacy. It only helps with documentation compliance."

Why say the AI limit out loud: it turns a safety constraint into a trust point. Administrators are rightly wary of AI touching clinical decisions.

07

Daily Log — the everyday workhorse

2 min
Menu→📝 Daily Log

Do: Add one entry live. Pick a resident, choose "Food intake," save it. Then switch the type dropdown to show narrative, vitals, bowel, hygiene.

Say"This is the day-to-day charting: shift narratives, vitals, food intake, bowel movements, hygiene. The form changes based on what you're recording, so a caregiver can chart in under 30 seconds from a phone. Every entry is timestamped — this is your evidence trail."

Why demo this live: speed of entry is the selling point. Charting friction is why paper logs go incomplete.

08

Incidents with built-in DSS guidance

2 min
Menu→🚨 Incidents→+ Report incident

Do: Open the incident form. Select "Fall" as the type, and point at the guidance tip that appears.

Say"Watch — when I choose the incident type, the app tells me the notification requirements: physician within 24 hours, family, whether DSS reporting is required. It even pre-sets the DSS-reportable field for things like elopement or death. Nobody has to memorize §87211 at 2 a.m."
09

Compliance checklist

1 min
Menu→✅ Compliance

Do: Check one item off. Show the categories.

Say"This is your standing inspection-readiness list, organized the way DSS actually reviews a facility. Work it down and you walk into an inspection already knowing the answer."
10

Ask the AI: "Am I ready for DSS?"

2 min
Menu→AI Assistant

Do: Tap the "Am I ready for DSS?" suggestion. Let the answer generate on screen.

Say"This is the question every administrator asks themselves. The AI reads your real records — certifications, incidents, checklist, MAR — and answers based on your data, not generic advice. It's like having a consultant who already read your files."

Why this is the closer: it ties every module you just showed into one answer. End the tour here — it's the peak.

11

Team roles & pricing — mention, don't dwell

1 min
Menu→Team/Billing
Say"You can invite your whole team — caregivers see only what they need for charting, supervisors see more, and you control it all. There's a free trial, plans scale with your facility, and you can manage or cancel billing yourself anytime — no phone calls."

The one-sentence close

"Everything an analyst will ask you for — staffing, training, medications, incidents, daily care — documented as you go, in one place, with an AI that tells you what's missing. That's Title22."

Then stop talking and hand them the phone. Letting them tap around sells better than any script.

Questions you'll get — and answers

"Where is our data stored? Is it secure?"
In a dedicated cloud database with row-level security — each facility's data is isolated, and access requires an authenticated login. Role permissions control what each staff member can see.
"Can my caregivers mess something up?"
Caregivers get a limited view: dashboard, MAR, incidents, and Daily Log. They can't delete records or touch billing, staff files, or facility settings.
"Does the AI make care decisions?"
No — by design. It never comments on dosages or clinical choices. It only reads your documentation and flags compliance gaps.
"What does it cost?"
There's a free trial to start, and monthly plans after that. Show them the Billing tab rather than reciting numbers — prices are current there.
"What if I stop paying — do I lose my records?"
Your data stays in your account. You can come back, and exports can be arranged. (Honest answer today; a self-serve export button is on the roadmap.)
